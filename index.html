Who is Mary?

Where do I come from?

Born on the Gold Coast in Australia, I was brought up in a beautiful city flourishing from the real estate and tourism industry.

I wasn't interested in a retirement lifestyle. I was bored and wanted to be where there was more learning, growth, inspiration and challenge, where perhaps I was connected to the greater world and it's communities. I was interested in nutrition and psychology so I decided to move an hour north and study in Brisbane. It was whilst studying Health Sciences that I found work at the local startup studio, Pitchblak.

Creative entrepreneurship

I began blending my learnings from nutrition and psychology, with my learnings at work in business stratgey, social media marketing, lead generation and operations. Everything cross-pollinated and I started visualising ways to make health care better, starting by focusing on people. I went to meetups and Student Market Days at Health Colleges where I pitched ideas, asked questions, and collected emails for web platform ideas.

With just enough insight, empathy and ideas, I won my first Startup Weekend in March 2016 back when I didn't even know a line of code. I did, however, deeply understand the problems from within nutrition clinics, where current software may be lagging or lacking. With a group of hackers and hustlers, we built an app that streamlined nutritional analysis by tapping into Clarafai's image recognition API.

Coding and Hacking

Since then I've been hooked and have been learning programming with my friends in Brisbane.

This is me at attended Django Girls learning Python and Git.

And this is me at a Code Retreat in Bali learning Html, CSS and Javascript.

I also attend and organise tech and startup events in Brisbane. I organised a screening of CODE: Debugging the Gender Gap at our local cinemas and I hosted a Q&A panel afterwards with Brisbane startup founders, entrepreneurs and programmers.

Dancing, Jamming and Painting

When I'm not coding, what do I get up to? I am probably biking, painting, reading or dancing. I use to do a lot of salsa dancing but have expanded to do more hip hop these days.

This is me dancing salsa in Bali.

I commited to learning hip hop for 2 months and signing up for my first on stage performance at the Judith Wright Center in Brisbane and then back stage afterwards.

I had started learning javascript at the same time that I started learning hip hop. I'd reasoned that the best way to learn something challenging was to throw myself in and totally immerse myself even if I feel like a dork with hip hop moves. With hip hop that meant to commitming myself to 3 or 4 hip hop classes a week and signing up to a performance in 2 months time. With web development that meant learning basic html, css and javascript and committimg myself to learning enough to attend a programming bootcamp like Galvanize.
